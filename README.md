## About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the **Industrial Robotics Facility (INBOT)** of the **Italian Institute of Technology (IIT)**, where I develop control methods for complex robotic systems.

My research lies at the intersection of **model-based control**, **reinforcement learning** and **experimental robotics**. I develop hybrid control architectures that combine the structure and reliability of established control methods with the adaptability of learning-based approaches.

> **Current focus:** tendon-driven continuum robots for the manipulation of soft and deformable objects.

---

## Research Focus

| Area | Main topics |
|---|---|
| **Robotics & Control** | Model-based control, hybrid control architectures and motion control |
| **Robot Learning** | Reinforcement learning, learning from demonstration and data-driven modelling |
| **Continuum Robotics** | Tendon-driven mechanisms, antagonistic actuation and shape control |
| **Robotic Perception** | Computer vision, 3D perception, visual feedback and sensor integration |
| **Industrial Robotics** | Collaborative robotics, robot integration and industrial automation |
| **Experimental Robotics** | System identification, validation, calibration and real-world deployment |

---

## Engineering Approach

My work spans the complete robotics pipeline:

**Mechanical design** → **System integration** → **Perception** → **Control** → **Experimental validation**

I approach robotics as an engineer: models and algorithms must ultimately work on the real physical system.
